Biography

I am a Ph.D. candidate and currently working as a research assistant at the Risk Decision Making Lab in the University of Alabama. I earned my MSc and BSc degrees from Jagannath University, Bangladesh. My research primarily focuses on natural hazard risk assessment, particularly in flood risk assessment. My ultimate research goal is to develop novel methodologies to simulate future flood risk by employing machine learning (ML) models along with GIS and Remote Sensing techniques. My work is distinguished by its novel approach, which comprehensively assesses flood risk utilizing ML models by incorporating both the physical dimensions and the social dimensions of floods. Remarkably, I have worked on flood susceptibility and risk in major Gulf Coast cities such as Mobile, New Orleans, Houston, and Tampa, currently published in peer review academic journals. Now I am moving forward to simulate flood risk for the US Gulf and Atlantic Coast. Additionally, I have served as a teaching assistant for one semester. Apart from his academic pursuits, he enjoys traveling, playing games, and photography in his leisure time.
